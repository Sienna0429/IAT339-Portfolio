157 Moments User Experience Design

Sienna Wu March 2025

Overview

Who is 157 Moments?

A fashion boutique located in Gastown, offering a carefully curated selection of unique items from around the world, ranging from women's clothing to perfumes and lifestyle products. They place great emphasis on the in-store user experience and offline interactions with customers.

Our Goal

Enhance users' in-store experience by redesigning the interaction model between users and the store.

Process

01

Ethnography

Through more in-depth interviews with them, participation in events, and observation of interactions during events, we narrowed down the broad goal of improving user experience into 3 specific concentrations:

  • Limited Product Information
  • Inconsistent Event Participation
  • Low Brand Awareness

However, since we didn't get enough data from the customers, we still lacked a deep understanding of the customer experience.

02

Persona

When analysing personas, we found that both staff and customers wanted more meaningful interactions with each other, though, not way too much.
Since the current event process is not well organised, some consumers may feel confused and bored during the event. And the lack of sufficient product information is indeed one of the key factors in the degradation of the customer experience.

Key decisions

  • To address the problem of limited product information, we planned to add information prompts to reduce customer confusion.
  • To encourage event engagement, we planned to create more meaningful connections among attendees and between customers and staff
  • We decided to drop the Low Brand Awareness concentration, because it leans more toward business and marketing strategy with limited space for interaction design. Our main focus for this project is solving design problems, thus, this pain point didn't align with our vision

03

User Journey

By stepping into the perspectives of The Browser and Event Participants, we gained insight into their experiences, thoughts, and frustrations at different stages rather than making assumptions.
We found that:

  • The Browser struggles with a lack of product information, making it difficult to understand pricing and brand narratives.
  • Event Participants may feel disengaged due to a rigid event structure with limited opportunities for interaction.

Key decisions

We have come up with more specific ideas to address the lack of information on products and the low participation in events.

  • To provide customers with more information, we could add price tag on each item or create a brand story booklet to offer a richer narrative about the brand.
  • To enhence event partipation, we were considering to incorporate interactive sessions to encourage social interaction

Takeaway:
User journey mapping isn't a one-time process

Although actions, thoughts, and feelings are listed, their logical relationships and how they influence each other are not clearly shown, leading to a misalignment in design focus.

04

Storyboard

Brand & Style Look-book

The brand look-book display is a visually engaging showcase that introduces customers to the brand's identity while offering real outfit inspirations.By featuring curated looks and brand narratives, it connects personal style with the brand's essence. Customers can just walk into the store, pick up an item, go through the brand look-book and get inspired to style it!

Scent Notes

TThe Scent Notes sharing book is an interactive in-store experience designed to develop a sense of connection through fragrance. By inviting customers to explore signature scents and record their impressions, the book becomes a collective sensory journal. As people read others' reflections, they gain new perspectives on the same scent, turning fragrance into a shared language that transcends individuality.

Pick & Seek Machine

Participants draw a random card from the Pick & Seek machine, each colour-coded to represent different product categories and featuring a descriptive clue. The challenge is to locate the corresponding item in the store, turning shopping into an interactive treasure hunt. Those who successfully find the item can select an outfit of their choice and have their moment captured with a Polaroid photo as a keepsake.

How Storyboard Hfelped?

Visualising User Experience
  • By creating a storyboard, we were able to map out the customer journey for each proposed concept. This process helped us anticipate how customers might react to our concepts, ensuring that these ideas aligned with their expectations.
Identifying Feasibility and Practicality
  • At first, we thought adding price tags would make product information clearer, but through storyboarding, we realized that constantly updating prices would be too much work for them. We'll look for a more practical solution going forward.

05

Workshop

We had a workshop with two of 157Moments' store staff to discuss the practical feasibility of the current ideas, identify Alternative Solutions to Overcome Potential Challenges, and explore more creative possibilities.

Avtivity 1: Brainstorm

In brainstorming session, participants spent 20 minutes to generate as many ideas as possible around the question:
"What makes a great event experience?"

Avtivity 2: Solution Ideation

After introducing our three design concepts, we created a mind map to identify concerns and possible solutions based on participant feedback. We'd like to gain more valuable insights from their perspectives.

Activity 3: Concept Visualisation

Booklet Collage

In part 1, participants spent 20 minutes creating collages to explore the possible lookbook style. This method helped us visualize abstract ideas, making them easier to understand.

Pick & Seek Card Rapid Prototypes

In part 2, Participants made rapid prototypes for Pick & Seek tasks and prompts. This helped us understand how to design engaging challenges that align with the store’s style.

What We Gathered

Employee Insights on Customer Behaviour

During the workshop, Nil and Ula shared their experiences interacting with customers in the store, giving us a broader perspective on how different customers react and think. For example, some might feel nervous about starting a conversation, while others may be too shy to try a perfume.

Design Considerations for Customer Engagement

The insights made us realise the importance of considering these behaviours in our design, such as adding prompts to encourage customers to engage with the staff. We also got some new ideas from the staff, such as considering a more engaging video format instead of a booklet.
