157 Moments - Enhancing In-Store Engagement through Interactive Design

Role: UX Researcher, Interaction Designer
Tools: Figma, Illustrator, Pen & Paper
Timeline: January 2025 – April 2025
Team: Sienna Wu, Xinzhe Pan, Jami Gandhi

Overview

Project Background

157 Moments is a fashion boutique located in Gastown, Vancouver, specializing in a curated selection of independent designer brands from around the world. The brand places a strong emphasis on in-store experiences, aiming to communicate its aesthetic and philosophy through meaningful offline interactions with customers. The goal of this project was to enhance the in-store experience through interaction design, fostering greater customer engagement and emotional connection.

My Role

I led communication with the store owner to align design goals and managed progress updates throughout the project. I conducted user interviews, on-site visits, and ethnographic observation to identify customer pain points, and designed low-to-mid fidelity prototypes and final visual deliverables such as posters and concept materials. I also facilitated a design workshop to validate our proposals with stakeholders.

Process

01

Ethnographic Research

I conducted field research at 157 Moments over two visits in two weeks. Through a one-hour semi-structured interview with the store manager, we learned about the brand's values and in-store goals. We also held an unstructured interview with the co-owner and two staff members to uncover internal perspectives and identify design focus areas.

Additionally, we carried out on-site observations at the Gastown location to explore the spatial experience and study how customers interacted with the store environment and staff.

Key Insights

01 – Opportunity for Digital Brand Storytelling

Many customers are curious about the stories and design philosophies behind the brands, but the store currently lacks ways to communicate them effectively.

04 – Value of Interactive Experiences

Interactive activities enhance customer experience and offer more natural opportunities for staff to engage in conversation.

02 – Need for Styling Support

Customers often feel unsure how to style certain items and seek simple, visual inspiration or guidance during their visit.

05 – Encouraging Conversations Through Prompts

Small prompts can reduce hesitation and encourage customers to interact more comfortably with staff.

03 – Challenges of Updating Printed Materials

Frequent product turnover makes it difficult for printed content like look-books to stay relevant or useful.

06 – Deepening Product Understanding Through Play

Interactive elements help customers explore and understand products more deeply through engaging, hands-on learning.

04

Design Concepts

DIGITAL LOOK BOOK

To address customers’ desire for more styling guidance and brand storytelling, the Digital Look-book presents curated outfit inspirations that reflect the brand's aesthetic. Customers can pick up an iPad from the table to explore these looks at their own pace. This allows them to better understand how to style individual pieces, sparking ideas and building confidence. When a particular style resonates with them, they can approach staff for help locating matching items like bags, scarves, or jewellery—fostering lightweight but meaningful interactions.

This concept responds to the insight that customers often seek styling advice and want to feel more connected to the brand story.

PICK AND SEEK

Pick & Seek turns shopping into an engaging, interactive experience. Customers draw a card with a creative prompt—such as “Find something that makes you feel confident”—and explore the store to find a matching item. This gamified experience encourages playful discovery and breaks down social barriers, creating more opportunities for organic staff-customer conversations. After styling their chosen item, customers can capture the moment with a Polaroid photo, leaving with both a look and a memory.

This concept responds to insights about the value of customer participation, the need for low-barrier conversation starters, and the importance of deepening product engagement.

Projet Takeaways

Through this project, I learned how to balance user needs with business constraints—an essential skill in real-world UX work. Engaging directly with store staff and observing in-person interactions revealed how powerful contextual research can be in shaping grounded and meaningful design directions.

Our co-creation workshop validated the value of interactive experiences that not only align with the brand's aesthetic but also fit within their operational realities. This experience deepened my understanding of the full UX process—from exploratory research and insight synthesis to concept development and stakeholder validation.

Throughout the project, I strengthened my skills in:

  • User-centered design thinking
  • Stakeholder communication
  • Visual storytelling (e.g., storyboards, journey maps)
  • Translating qualitative insights into design decisions

Most importantly, I learned how to bridge creativity with feasibility—ensuring that the solutions I propose are not just engaging and user-friendly, but also practical and implementable within the constraints of a real business.
