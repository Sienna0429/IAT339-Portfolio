157 Moments User Experience Design

Sienna Wu March 2025

Overview

Who is 157 Moments?

A fashion boutique located in Gastown, offering a carefully curated selection of unique items from around the world, ranging from women's clothing to perfumes and lifestyle products. They place great emphasis on the in-store user experience and offline interactions with customers.

Our Goal

Enhance users' in-store experience by redesigning the interaction model between users and the store.

Process

01

Ethnography

Through more in-depth interviews with them, participation in events, and observation of interactions during events, we narrowed down the broad goal of improving user experience into 3 specific concentrations:

  • Limited Product Information
  • Inconsistent Event Participation
  • Low Brand Awareness

However, since we didn't get enough data from the customers, we still lacked a deep understanding of the customer experience.

02

Persona

When analysing personas, we found that both staff and customers wanted more meaningful interactions with each other, though, not way too much.
Since the current event process is not well organised, some consumers may feel confused and bored during the event. And the lack of sufficient product information is indeed one of the key factors in the degradation of the customer experience.

Key decisions

  • To address the problem of limited product information, we planned to add information prompts to reduce customer confusion.
  • To encourage event engagement, we planned to create more meaningful connections among attendees and between customers and staff
  • We decided to drop the Low Brand Awareness concentration, because it leans more toward business and marketing strategy with limited space for interaction design. Our main focus for this project is solving design problems, thus, this pain point didn't align with our vision

03

User Journey

By stepping into the perspectives of The Browser and Event Participants, we gained insight into their experiences, thoughts, and frustrations at different stages rather than making assumptions.
We found that:

  • The Browser struggles with a lack of product information, making it difficult to understand pricing and brand narratives.
  • Event Participants may feel disengaged due to a rigid event structure with limited opportunities for interaction.

Key decisions

We have come up with more specific ideas to address the lack of information on products and the low participation in events.

  • To provide customers with more information, we could add price tag on each item or create a brand story booklet to offer a richer narrative about the brand.
  • To enhence event partipation, we were considering to incorporate interactive sessions to encourage social interaction

Takeaway:
User journey mapping isn't a one-time process

Although actions, thoughts, and feelings are listed, their logical relationships and how they influence each other are not clearly shown, leading to a misalignment in design focus.
