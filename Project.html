157 Moments - Enhancing In-Store Engagement through Interactive Design

Sienna Wu March 2025

Overview

Project Background

157 Moments is a fashion boutique located in Gastown, Vancouver, specializing in a curated selection of independent designer brands from around the world. The brand places a strong emphasis on in-store experiences, aiming to communicate its aesthetic and philosophy through meaningful offline interactions with customers. The goal of this project was to enhance the in-store experience through interaction design, fostering greater customer engagement and emotional connection.

My Role

I led communication with the store owner to align design goals and managed progress updates throughout the project. I conducted user interviews, on-site visits, and ethnographic observation to identify customer pain points, and designed low-to-mid fidelity prototypes and final visual deliverables such as posters and concept materials. I also facilitated a design workshop to validate our proposals with stakeholders.

Process

01

Ethnographic Research

I conducted in-depth interviews with the owner and staff, and observed multiple store events. While these helped us identify key issues—such as limited product information, inconsistent event participation, and low brand awareness—I recognized that direct customer data was lacking, which became a turning point in refocusing our design scope.

03

Design Concepts

DIGITAL LOOK BOOK

The Digital Look-book showcases curated styles that connect customers with the brand's identity. By picking up an iPad from the tables, they can explore outfit inspirations, get inspired, and ask about specific pieces. Staff can help them find matching items like bag, scarves or jewellery, making styling more personalised.

PICK AND SEEK

Pick & Seek makes shopping fun and interactive. Customers draw a card with a clue and search for the matching item in the store. It's a game that encourages curiosity, discovery, and connection. When they find the item, they can try it on, style it, and take a Polaroid photo as a keepsake. It turns a regular shopping trip into a playful, memorable experience.

How Storyboard Helped?

Visualising User Experience
  • By creating a storyboard, we were able to map out the customer journey for each proposed concept. This process helped us anticipate how customers might react to our concepts, ensuring that these ideas aligned with their expectations.
Identifying Feasibility and Practicality
  • At first, we thought adding price tags would make product information clearer, but through storyboarding, we realized that constantly updating prices would be too much work for them. We'll look for a more practical solution going forward.

05

Workshop

We had a workshop with two of 157Moments' store staff to discuss the practical feasibility of the current ideas, identify Alternative Solutions to Overcome Potential Challenges, and explore more creative possibilities.

Avtivity 1: Brainstorm

In brainstorming session, participants spent 20 minutes to generate as many ideas as possible around the question:
"What makes a great event experience?"

Avtivity 2: Solution Ideation

After introducing our three design concepts, we created a mind map to identify concerns and possible solutions based on participant feedback. We'd like to gain more valuable insights from their perspectives.

Activity 3: Concept Visualisation

Booklet Collage

In part 1, participants spent 20 minutes creating collages to explore the possible lookbook style. This method helped us visualize abstract ideas, making them easier to understand.

Pick & Seek Card Rapid Prototypes

In part 2, Participants made rapid prototypes for Pick & Seek tasks and prompts. This helped us understand how to design engaging challenges that align with the store’s style.

What We Gathered

Employee Insights on Customer Behaviour

During the workshop, Nil and Ula shared their experiences interacting with customers in the store, giving us a broader perspective on how different customers react and think. For example, some might feel nervous about starting a conversation, while others may be too shy to try a perfume.

Design Considerations for Customer Engagement

The insights made us realise the importance of considering these behaviours in our design, such as adding prompts to encourage customers to engage with the staff. We also got some new ideas from the staff, such as considering a more engaging video format instead of a booklet.

Outcome & Reflection

Our workshop with the brand confirmed strong interest in interactive experiences that fit the store’s aesthetic and operational reality. This project deepened my understanding of the UX process, from exploratory research to synthesizing insights into actionable design. I developed stronger skills in: User-centered design thinking Communication with stakeholders Visual storytelling (e.g. storyboards, journey maps) Translating qualitative insights into design direction Most importantly, I learned to balance feasibility with creativity—ensuring that my design solutions are not only engaging, but also implementable in real-world settings.
